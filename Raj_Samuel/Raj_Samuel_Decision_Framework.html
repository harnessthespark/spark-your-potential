This framework helps you evaluate ANY opportunity against your patterns. Use it whenever a new role emergesâ€”whether agency, client-side, consultancy, or your own venture.

Key insight from our work: You need environments that recognise the importance of culture above all else. You're not just doing strategy - you're creating cultural and organisational transformation.

The Four Core Tests

Any opportunity should pass these tests to be worth your energy:

1. The Cultural Transformation Test

Core Question: Will this let me do cultural transformationâ€”not just strategy delivery?

  • Can I spot where culture's already moved and make the path visible?
  • Will I be valued for making the invisible visible?
  • Is this an environment that values cultural truth over surface solutions?
  • Can I bridge external and internal culture (not just deliver decks)?
  • Will I have space to dig in places others aren't looking?

If you're boxed into "digital strategist" or just making ads without cultural depth, this fails the test.

2. The Permission Test

Core Question: Will I be able to give people permission to be brave?

  • Can I ask questions about culture, emotion, play, and fun?
  • Will my "Of Course" moments be valued - making people see what was already there?
  • Can I help people reframe problems rather than just solve the stated brief?
  • Will I have permission to show where culture's already gone?
  • Is there appetite for the "scary thing" that feels obvious in hindsight?

If you can't give permission or receive it, this fails the test.

3. The Space to Teach Test

Core Question: Will I have space to teach and educate?

  • Can I run workshops that create belief (like Cadbury)?
  • Will I have time to show people how content actually lives in the world now?
  • Is there room to build capabilities in others, not just deliver?
  • Can I help shift thinking, not just hand over strategy decks?
  • Will people value learning to see patterns, not just receiving answers?

If it's just delivery with no education element, this fails the test.

4. The Freedom to Create Test

Core Question: Will I have freedom to create and explore?

  • Can I go down rabbit holes when I sense something interesting?
  • Will experimentation be valued (like Malice Arts experiential approach)?
  • Is there room for interactivity, participation, play?
  • Can I add my own perspective rather than just execute others' visions?
  • Will I have autonomy to spot opportunities others don't see?

If everything is pre-defined with no room for curiosity, this fails the test.

Questions to Ask in Interviews

About Cultural Transformation:

  • "How does this organisation view the relationship between culture and strategy?"
  • "Can you give an example of when the team helped a brand catch up to where culture had already moved?"
  • "Is this more about delivering strategies or transforming how people think?"
  • "How much time is there to dig into cultural truths vs. just executing briefs?"

About Permission & Courage:

  • "Tell me about a time when someone here helped a client do something brave - something that felt obvious in hindsight?"
  • "What happens when a recommendation challenges the stated brief?"
  • "How do you help clients see things they couldn't articulate before?"
  • "Is there appetite for the 'scary thing' that needs doing?"

About Teaching & Creating:

  • "How much of this role is about building capabilities in clients vs. just delivering?"
  • "Is there space for workshops and education - or is it purely consulting?"
  • "How does the team feel about going down rabbit holes when they sense something interesting?"
  • "What role does play, interactivity, and experimentation have here?"
